FAQs

  • What is Psychotherapy?

    The way mental illness or psychological disorder is treated is called Psychotherapy. This is a process through which a therapist takes sessions with a client suffering from mental illness.

  • Who needs psychotherapy?

    Those people who are facing problems in dealing with their daily lives. Some of the patients feel anxiety or depression and have serious psychological disorders. Through therapy, they can cure their problems.

  • Types of Psychotherapy-

    Different types of psychotherapy are used by mental health professionals. those techniques are-
    ● Cognitive Behavioural Technique ( CBT):The therapist focuses on the patient's behaviour in this technique. In this therapy, The unhealthy thought patterns are replaced with the exact thought pattern.
    ● Interpersonal Therapy:Those patients deal with their personal grief and anxiety. This therapy helps them to release their emotions. ● Psychodynamic Therapy:This therapy helps break the old childhood thought pattern to free themselves.
    Apart from those, there are various types of Psychotherapies.

  • Where does Psychotherapy help?

    Therapy has immense power in that it can heal a person. Different mental issues can create problems for people. Some of them are-
    ● Any kind of Addiction.
    ● Bipolar disorder.
    ● Depression.
    ● Eating Disorders.
    ● Traumas.
    ● Phobias.

  • Benefits of Psychotherapy-

    When a person deals with mental issues, life becomes difficult for them. As it is an implicit problem that others hardly understand. In this case, psychotherapy works like wonder. After therapy, one can have-
    ● Positive views through life.
    ● Great bonds with close people.
    ● Energetic in dealing with problems, etc.

  • Is Psychotherapy worthy?

    There are various questions regarding this topic. Does Psychotherapy work? In Researcher's study, it has been shown that psychotherapy does not show changes physically. However, it creates immense effects on the brain. People get actual benefits from the therapy that medication cannot achieve.
